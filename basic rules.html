Basic rules of Floorball

Note: The rules are categorised for easier reference. Click on any category to read more about the rules related to it.

The court

  • The court measures 20m by 40m.
  • Boards surrounding the court are usually made of heavy duty plastic, composites or high density foam that measure 50 cm high.
  • Total duration of game consist of three 20-minute periods.

The players

  • A full team typically consists of 18 stick players and 2 goalies.
  • At any point during the game, each side has 5 stick players and 1 goalie on court.
  • Floorball adopts a "Rolling Substitution" - Players are free to substitute anytime and however many times as they please. The game will not stop during any substitution.

Restrictions during the game

  • No high sticking. Blade must stay below player's knees. If ball is played with any part of the stick above the knee, it is high sticking.
  • No slashing. Do not hit your opponent's stick.
  • No lifting, pushing or kicking the opponent's stick.
  • You cannot reach between your opponent's legs with your stick.
  • Do not play the ball with your hand or head.
  • Do not jump to reach the ball.

Some offences leading to free-hits

  • When a player hits, blocks, lifts, kicks an opponent’s stick.
  • When a player, in control of the ball, or trying to reach it, forces or pushes an opponent in any way other than shoulder to shoulder.
  • When a field player intentionally moves the opposing team’s goal cage.
  • When a field player plays the ball with his head.

Positions in Floorball

The basic positions in a team are: a goalkeeper, two defenders, a centre and two attackers. This table summarises the positions and their roles in Floorball:

Position Description
Forward During offence, the attacker is responsible for creating an opening to receive the ball, and shooting the ball into the goal. During defense, the attackers will prevent the opponents' defenders from bringing their play up. They do so by pressing the defenders and cutting their passes.
Centre The centre is responsible for supporting the attack and to support the defence. This means that centres are required to run a lot and have an excellent sense of positioning.
Defender During offence, the defender is responsible for bringing the play up. They do so by passing the ball to the center / forwards directly. During defense, the defender is responsible for preventing the opponents' attackers from scoring. They do so by blocking shots, stealing the ball away and cutting passes between attackers.
Goalkeeper The goalkeeper's primary role is to prevent the opponent from scoring. Fast reflexes Fast reflexes may be what distinguishes the best goalkeepers from the rest.
